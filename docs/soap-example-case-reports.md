# SOAP Synthetic Case Report Examples

These five examples show how the demo can turn patient answers, history, and
available objective records into a physician-review draft. They are synthetic
teaching examples only. They are not clinical advice, not final diagnoses, not
orders, and not treatment instructions.

## Case 1 - BPH / LUTS Review Pattern

Common symptom context: urinary frequency, nocturia, weak stream, hesitancy.

### S - Subjective

- 65-year-old male reports urinary frequency and nocturia for 6 months.
- Wakes 4 to 5 times per night to urinate.
- Reports weak stream, hesitancy, and incomplete emptying sensation.
- Symptoms gradually became more bothersome during the past 2 months.
- Denies fever, gross hematuria, flank pain, and unintentional weight loss.
- Past history: hypertension and hyperlipidemia.
- Medication history: amlodipine reported by patient.
- Allergy history: no known drug allergy reported.
- Family history: father had prostate enlargement history.
- Social history: non-smoker; occasional alcohol use.

### O - Objective

- BP 138/82 mmHg, HR 76 bpm, Temp 36.7 C.
- Digital rectal examination note in record: enlarged smooth prostate,
  non-tender.
- Urinalysis: no pyuria and no hematuria in the available record.
- PSA: 2.4 ng/mL in the available record.
- Bladder ultrasound: enlarged prostate and post-void residual urine 90 mL in
  the available record.

### A - Assessment

- Physician-review draft: lower urinary tract symptom pattern with storage and
  voiding complaints.
- Current draft should be reviewed with history, examination, medication list,
  and available objective records before final documentation.

### P - Plan

- Physician may confirm symptom severity, bother score, medication history, and
  red-flag history during the visit.
- Physician may decide whether additional objective records or follow-up items
  are needed under local clinic workflow.
- Patient-facing output should remain limited to previsit information
  collection and clinician review.

## Case 2 - UTI-Like Symptom Review Pattern

Common symptom context: dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic discomfort.

### S - Subjective

- 28-year-old female reports dysuria and urinary frequency for 3 days.
- Describes burning sensation during urination and urgency.
- Mild suprapubic discomfort developed today.
- Denies flank pain, fever, nausea, and gross hematuria.
- Past history: two patient-reported UTI episodes during the previous year.
- Medication history: no regular medication reported.
- Allergy history: penicillin allergy reported.
- Social history: sexually active.

### O - Objective

- BP 112/70 mmHg, HR 88 bpm, Temp 37.4 C.
- Abdominal examination note in record: mild suprapubic tenderness.
- Urinalysis record: leukocyte esterase positive, nitrite positive, elevated
  WBC.
- Urine culture status in record: pending.

### A - Assessment

- Physician-review draft: urinary pain and frequency pattern with available
  urinalysis findings.
- Final interpretation should remain with the physician after reviewing
  symptoms, allergy history, pregnancy-related context when applicable, and
  culture follow-up status.

### P - Plan

- Physician may confirm fever, flank pain, pregnancy-related context, allergy
  details, and prior episode history.
- Physician may decide the visit documentation, follow-up, and patient
  instructions under clinic workflow.
- Patient should not receive automated diagnosis or medication instructions from
  the demo.

## Case 3 - Kidney Stone / Renal Colic Review Pattern

Common symptom context: sudden flank pain radiating to groin, nausea,
microscopic hematuria.

### S - Subjective

- 42-year-old male reports sudden severe right flank pain for 6 hours.
- Pain radiates toward the groin and is associated with nausea and sweating.
- Denies fever and dysuria.
- Past history: patient reports a kidney stone episode 5 years ago.
- Medication history: no regular medication reported.
- Social history: poor water intake and high-salt diet reported.

### O - Objective

- BP 150/92 mmHg, HR 102 bpm, Temp 36.8 C.
- Examination note in record: right costovertebral angle tenderness.
- Urinalysis record: microscopic hematuria.
- CT record: 5 mm right distal ureter stone and mild hydronephrosis.

### A - Assessment

- Physician-review draft: acute flank pain pattern with radiating pain and
  available imaging record.
- Physician should review pain control needs, fever status, kidney function,
  and imaging details before final documentation.

### P - Plan

- Physician may confirm pain severity, fever, urine output, renal function, and
  medication safety context.
- Physician may decide clinic or emergency workflow actions based on current
  examination and local protocol.
- The demo should only surface the structured previsit record and review flags.

## Case 4 - Painless Gross Hematuria Review Pattern

Common symptom context: painless visible blood in urine.

### S - Subjective

- 58-year-old male reports painless gross hematuria since yesterday.
- Urine appears dark red without clots according to patient report.
- Denies dysuria, fever, flank pain, and trauma.
- Past history: smoking history of 30 pack-years.
- Medication history: aspirin reported by patient.
- Family history: father had bladder cancer.

### O - Objective

- Vital signs in available note: stable.
- Abdominal examination note: no abdominal tenderness.
- Flank examination note: no flank knocking pain.
- Urinalysis record: gross hematuria.
- CBC record: hemoglobin within reference range.
- CT urography status in record: pending.

### A - Assessment

- Physician-review draft: painless visible hematuria pattern with medication,
  smoking, and family history context.
- Physician should review malignancy-related, stone-related, infection-related,
  medication-related, and other relevant pathways according to clinical
  judgment.

### P - Plan

- Physician may confirm duration, clot history, urinary symptoms, anticoagulant
  or antiplatelet context, and available objective records.
- Physician may decide documentation, evaluation pathway, and counseling under
  local clinic workflow.
- The demo should mark visible hematuria for clinician review without automatic
  disease labeling.

## Case 5 - Urinary Retention Review Pattern

Common symptom context: inability to urinate, suprapubic fullness, prior weak
stream and hesitancy.

### S - Subjective

- 72-year-old male reports inability to urinate for 8 hours.
- Reports severe lower abdominal fullness and discomfort.
- During the past several months, patient noticed weak stream, hesitancy, and
  nocturia.
- Past history: diabetes mellitus and prostate enlargement history reported.
- Medication history: antihypertensive medication reported.

### O - Objective

- BP 162/94 mmHg, HR 96 bpm, Temp 36.9 C.
- Lower abdomen in available examination note: distended with suprapubic
  tenderness.
- Bladder scan record: retained urine volume approximately 900 mL.
- Laboratory record: mildly elevated creatinine.

### A - Assessment

- Physician-review draft: acute inability-to-void pattern with suprapubic
  fullness and available bladder scan record.
- Physician should review current urination status, discomfort severity, renal
  function, medication context, and prior lower urinary tract symptoms before
  final documentation.

### P - Plan

- Physician may confirm current voiding status and decide the immediate clinic
  workflow under local protocol.
- Physician may decide whether further documentation, monitoring, or follow-up
  items are needed.
- The demo should surface this as a clinician-review flag, not an automated
  treatment instruction.
